# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fashion and creative industry contacts — brands, collaborators, clients, and press — evaluating Malaolu's work and Suavee Studios. Arrive primarily from Instagram and direct links on mobile. Secondary: recruiters and prospective partners reaching him for art direction and styling engagements. They are visual-fluent, high-taste, impatient with noise. They make a fast judgment call; the site has one job — get out of the way and let the work earn it.

## Product Purpose

An editorial portfolio for Malaolu Abdulrazak — Creative Director, Art Director, Stylist, and founder of Suavee Studios. The site exists to communicate the quality and range of his creative vision: fashion, styling, art direction, and visual storytelling. Success means a visitor opens the Work PDF, visits Suavee, or reaches out via email. Everything else is context.

## Brand Personality

Quiet. Curatorial. Precise.

A site where the UI disappears and the work leads. Gallery energy, not agency energy. Like a physical archive someone has been building for years — not a pitch deck, not a reel, not a template. The scrapbook-editorial rhythm of byseanbrown.com is the structural reference: long justified image feed, imagery and text alternating without hierarchy competition, no chrome competing with the content.

## References

- **byseanbrown.com** — specifically: the long justified image feed, scrapbook-editorial rhythm, full-bleed images without decorative treatment, the way the UI disappears into the content. Structure and interactions only — no text, imagery, or branding.

## Anti-references

- Generic creative portfolio templates (Squarespace/Webflow defaults) — grid of cards with project titles and hover overlays. The work as product listing.
- LinkedIn-style professional showcase — keyword-heavy, corporate structure, credentials over voice.
- Flashy motion-heavy agency sites — gratuitous animation, self-congratulatory technology, the design louder than the work.
- The AI-default editorial-magazine aesthetic — Fraunces italic drop caps + broadsheet grid + monochromatic restraint + tiny tracked kickers above every section. This is the trap for any fashion/creative brief; it reads as costume, not identity.

## Design Principles

1. **The work leads, the UI disappears.** Every design decision is measured against whether it competes with the imagery. If it does, it loses.
2. **Curatorial restraint is not timidity.** Restraint is a committed POV. Blank space earns its keep; nothing decorative survives without a reason.
3. **Precision at every scale.** Malaolu's work is about attention to detail — garment construction, casting, set design. The site must reflect that sensibility: nothing approximate, nothing rounded off.
4. **One decisive image beats five safe ones.** Gallery logic. The feed is an edited sequence, not a dump.
5. **Mobile-first is non-negotiable.** The primary visitor is on a phone arriving from Instagram. Every layout decision is tested at 375px first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. All interactive elements keyboard-operable. Meaningful alt text on every image (the alt is part of the curatorial voice — "Petalos Playing Cards — Suavee Studios, S/S 2025" not "image"). Honor prefers-reduced-motion throughout. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against background.
